Research

How does agricultural management change soil carbon stocks over decades?
What are the microbiological, physical and chemical factors that drive these changes?

These are the questions underlying my graduate work at UC Davis, asked in the context of the unique Russell Ranch Century Experiment. As part of the Scow Soil Microbial Ecology Lab, I shed light on the processes that drive carbon loss and formation in surface and subsurface soils under practices such as cover cropping, compost and mineral N fertilizer application.
